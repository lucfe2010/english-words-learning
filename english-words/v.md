# verdict

**verdicts** 是英文单词 **verdict** 的**复数形式**。

下面给你一个详细、系统的拆解（发音 → 词源 → 核心含义 → 用法场景 → 常见搭配 → 例句）：

### 1. 发音
- **英式**：/ˈvɜː.dɪkt/  
  （第一音节重读，听起来像“**VUH**-dikt”）
- **美式**：/ˈvɝː.dɪkt/  
  （第一音节重读，更像“**VER**-dikt”，r音更明显）

复数 **verdicts** 发音基本不变，只在词尾加 /s/ → /ˈvɜː.dɪkts/ 或 /ˈvɝː.dɪkts/

### 2. 词源（非常有意思的“真话”来源）
verdict ← 中古英语 verdit ← 古法语 veirdit / voirdit  
← 拉丁语 **vērum dictum** = **vērum**（真的） + **dictum**（说的话、声明）

→ 字面意思就是 **“真实说出来的话” → “真相的宣告”**

后来受到中世纪拉丁文 **vērdictum** / **veredictum** 影响，拼写固定成现在的 **verdict**。

同根词（看到 ver- 基本都跟“真”有关）：
- verify（验证 = 使之成为真的）
- verity（真实、真理）
- very（最初意思也是“真的”）
- veracious（真实的、说真话的）

### 3. 核心含义（按使用频率排序）
1. **【法律】陪审团的裁决 / 判决结果**（最常见、最正式用法）  
   → 陪审团（jury）在审判结束后给出的正式结论

2. **【广义】判断、意见、定论、评价**（日常用法最多）  
   → 某人对某事的最终看法（常带点“经过思考/观察后”的感觉）

3. **【医学/调查】鉴定结论、审查结果**（较少见）  
   如：coroner's verdict（验尸官结论）

### 4. 常见分类（法律语境里最常出现的几种verdict）
| 英文表达               | 中文常见翻译         | 说明                              |
|-----------------------|----------------------|-----------------------------------|
| guilty verdict        | 有罪裁决             | 判定有罪                          |
| not guilty verdict    | 无罪裁决             | 判定无罪（注意不是“无罪证明”）   |
| innocent verdict      | 无罪判决             | （较少用，not guilty 更标准）     |
| hung jury / no verdict| 陪审团无法达成一致   | 无法做出裁决，审判可能作废        |
| open verdict          | 开放式裁决           | 死因不明 / 无法确定               |
| directed verdict      | 法官直接裁决         | 法官认为证据太明显，直接终止陪审  |

### 5. 典型搭配 & 短语
- reach/return/deliver a verdict  
  做出/宣布裁决
- the jury came back with a verdict  
  陪审团带回了裁决
- await the verdict  
  等待判决
- popular verdict / public verdict  
  公众舆论的判断
- the verdict is still out (on sth)  
  还没定论、尚未有结论（超级地道的表达！）
- get a fair/unfair verdict  
  得到公平/不公平的评价

### 6. 真实例句（由浅到深）
- The jury returned a **guilty verdict** after only four hours of deliberation.  
  （陪审团只商议了4小时就做出了有罪裁决。）

- What's your **verdict** on the new iPhone? — I think it's overpriced.  
  （你对新iPhone的评价如何？——我觉得定价太高了。）

- The **verdicts** in high-profile cases often spark heated public debate.  
  （备受关注的案件的判决常常引发激烈的公众争论。）

- After months of testing, the **verdict is still out** on whether this diet really works.  
  （经过几个月的测试，这个饮食法到底有没有效仍然没有定论。）

- The court of public opinion has already delivered its **verdict**: he's guilty.  
  （舆论法庭已经做出了判决：他有罪。）

一句话总结：  
**verdict** = “经过慎重考虑后说出的‘真话’/结论”，法庭里最严肃，生活中最常用。

# vigurous

## Phonetics & Classification

* **IPA:** UK: /ˈvɪɡ.ər.əs/ | US: /ˈvɪɡ.ər.əs/
* **CEFR Level:** B2/C1

---

## Core Meanings

* **English Definitions:**
* *Adjective:* Strong, healthy, and full of energy.
* *Adjective:* Characterized by or involving physical strength, effort, or energy.
* *Adjective:* (Of an action) forceful or intense.


* **Chinese Definitions:**
* *形容词 (adj.):* 精力充沛的；强壮的；有力的；（行动）激烈的。


* **Collocations:**
* **Vigorous exercise** (e.g., running, swimming)
* A **vigorous defense** of one's rights
* **Vigorous growth** (in plants or economy)
* **Vigorous shaking** / stirring



---

## Contextual Usage

* **Natural Example:** "After a **vigorous** workout at the gym, I usually feel exhausted but incredibly refreshed."
* **Formal/Academic Example:** "The proposed legislation faced **vigorous** opposition from environmental groups who argued it would accelerate deforestation."

---

## Linguistic Origins & Family & Memory Anchor

* **Etymology:**
* **Root:** Latin *vigor* (liveliness/force), from *vigere* (to be lively/thrive).


* **Word Family:**
* **Noun:** Vigor (US) / Vigour (UK)
* **Adverb:** Vigorously
* **Verb:** Invigorate (to give energy to)


* **Mnemonic:** Think of **Vigorous** = **V**ery **I**ntense **G**o. When you are vigorous, you have the "Vigor" to keep "Going" with high intensity.
* **Logical Association:** The root *vig-* is shared with "vegetable" (something that grows/thrives). Just as a healthy plant shows *vigorous* growth, a healthy person shows *vigorous* energy.

---

## Nuance & Distinction

* **Synonyms:**
* *Physical:* Energetic, robust, strenuous, athletic.
* *Argumentative/Abstract:* Forceful, spirited, vehement, dynamic.


* **Antonyms:**
* Feeble, weak, lethargic, frail, sluggish.


* **The "Don't Mix Up" Section: Vigorous vs. Strenuous**
* **Strenuous** describes something that *requires* great effort and often implies it is taxing or difficult (e.g., "a strenuous climb"). It focuses on the **difficulty**.
* **Vigorous** describes the **energy and force** put into the action. You can have a "vigorous debate" (passionate/active), but you wouldn't usually call a debate "strenuous" unless it was physically exhausting to participate in.
